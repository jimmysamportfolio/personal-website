I graduated high school fully convinced I was heading into finance. Spreadsheets, markets, the whole thing. But somewhere between graduation and my first semester, something shifted. I made a last-second pivot and enrolled in Business + Computer Science at UBC. Best decision I ever made.

## Product first.

My first real tech experience was in product management. I competed in the UBC PMC x TELUS Product Sprint, where our team built PRD+, a Chrome extension to streamline how TELUS product teams write and align on requirements. 

We won first place out of 20 teams. That experience taught me that great products start with asking why.

## Then engineering.

Product got me hooked, but I wanted to build things myself. I started entering hackathons and shipping real projects. At nwHacks 2026, our team built [Nexus](/project/nexus), an agentic event production platform, and won. 

<Image src="/about/nwHacks-win.jpg" alt="nwHacks win" width={1200} height={800} caption="Winning nwHacks 2026 with Nexus" />

At Advizr, a local AI consulting firm, I shipped agentic AI and RAG products to small businesses:
- Custom RAG pipeline for a real estate company,
- Automated data scraper for 10,000+ clinics across Canada.

I also helped develop infra that scaled to over 300 projects built and $10M+ in client revenue generated.

<Image src="/about/advizr.png" alt="Advizr Landing" width={1200} height={800} caption="Advizr Website" />

Now I'm at [Cedar AI Agents](https://www.getcedar.ai/) as a founding engineer, building with voice AI agents. We're currently in stealth.
